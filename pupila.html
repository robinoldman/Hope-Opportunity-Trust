Marselle

I've been able to experience life for what it is, which is a treasure box full of opportunities. It just taught me so much about myself, about what I could be interested in, and start building up all these worlds of possibilities. The confidence I have in myself to know that my education, how my ability is, with something someone saw that way within me was really great. I grew up in Blacon, and if you speak to people outside of Blacon and ask them their opinions on this place, usually you get quite negative comments.

CI was pulled into the head teacher's office. He told me about this opportunity Blacon High School had a partnership with RNSCF. It was a nine-month process where I went on taster visits to boarding schools. I went through an admissions process at Glenalmond. I had to get five Bs at GCSE, and then they placed you with the school. After that point, it was brought to me the opportunity to potentially go to Eton. The Opportunity Trust prepared me for that very well. I chose economics at A-level in Peters, which wouldn't have been available at Holly Lodge, so that was one thing that is a big difference. I never would have picked what I was doing now because I never would have had exposure to the things I'm learning about now. It's quite a hard thing to think about when you're 15. This transition from one place is completely different from another.

I was a little bit more homesick than I thought I was going to be, but I was quite confident in myself, and I knew that I would be able to maneuver my way through. Try to express myself in as many ways as possible. When I went to boarding, I realized, you know, it just sort of empowered me and involved me to be who I am. When I got to Eton, I realized that the world is my oyster. I was applying to schools that I didn't even dream of applying to. I managed to get a place at UCL, which is a top 10 university in the world. I've just put myself in a position where I've just ticked along, asked the right questions, and met the right people at the right times that has come to fruition.

In terms of future aspirations, I'm in quite a privileged position to be able to have options. Art is something that I'm quite interested in, and I've managed to secure an internship at the Tate Gallery this summer as a curator. You have the luxury of being able to choose from a different range of fields about your experiences, about in that way you think. That really opened my mind to what life is really like.

Ambitions

Our success to date has given us the momentum and motivation to do more. To change more lives and create more opportunities for our young people. To that end, we have identified seven key opportunities, but to make them happen we need to raise new independent funding. This would enable us to extend and enrich our work impacting on even more young people and their communities. We are asking for your support to make this happen.

We have a detailed and cost-effective budget that will enable us to deliver on our seven key opportunities. The total budget of circa £537.5k over 5 years will ensure the expansion and sustainability of the programme. and is a more than reasonable cost, balanced against the £2 million of "in kind" investment in students that we will generate over the same period.

The new funding and programmes will enable us to increase and develop life-changing opportunities for more young people and ensure our staff can extend the necessary support to families and communities to raise their levels of aspiration and achievement.

The seven key opportunities for expanding our impact are;

1. Direct access for students from the Northwest.

The Arnold Foundation, the charitable arm of Rugby College, is committed to working with HOT to increase access to Rugby College for several additional students from the North West.

We know from experience that supporting five students and their families from selection to placement and thereafter will require an increase in our core staff capacity by 25%. Working with The Arnold Foundation to support these students will therefore take an additional part-time role and support costs of circa £25 K p.a.

Total £125K over 5 years

2. Developing Apprenticeship Opportunities to expand the HOT offer to our Member schools and the community .

Collaborating with DLA Piper, a national firm of solicitors, and the Crown Prosecution Service, we have been given access to fully funded apprenticeships and supported university places for students who wish to pursue a career in the legal profession.

We have also been approached to deliver apprenticeships for the BBC, LA Productions, and the Anthony Walker Foundation.

Whilst we can distribute the places, it is vitally important that we can fully support the students throughout the apprenticeship programme to ensure their success. This will take an additional part-time role and support costs of circa £16.5K p.a.

Total £82.5K over 5 years

3 Parent mentoring programme

From the very beginning, we have recognised the need to support families and guardians. It is an investment that is vital to the success of the boarding programme, recognising that many parents have difficulty adjusting and giving their children the space and support to take advantage of this life-changing educational opportunity.

We know from experience that close collaboration with all parties helps minimise the risk of the “pull” from parents and the wider home community to “stick to what they are familiar with” Without this support, parents can struggle to overcome their concerns about losing their child to a world they are unfamiliar with and unsure how to support their child within it. For this to succeed it needs resourcing beyond the volunteer support that is currently available.

We want to develop a parent mentoring programme that mirrors the existing successful student mentoring programme, thereby linking new parents with mentors whose children have been through the process and are now in employment.

Total of £35K over 5 years

4 - Aspire and engage programme

We aim to increase the number of students receiving bursaries through HOT and its partners through the creation of the “Aspire and Engage” mentoring programme for Year 9 students that will support them to widen their horizons, raise and realise their ambitions and discover their self-worth.

By connecting them with mentors and role models from a wide range of professions we want to inspire and empower boys and girls from the least advantaged communities to develop the capacity to shape their world and their future by challenging stereotypes and expectations. In particular, we want to ensure boys see that their futures are not limited by their gender, ethnicity, background, parental income or expectation.

Successful candidates will receive nine 1:1 mentor sessions and undertake two specific research tasks throughout the year. The impact of the programme will be measured by a quantifiable improvement in school performance data, an increase in the number of girls and boys gaining bursaries and good quality higher education places and other education and employment pathways.

Total £82.5K over 5 years

5 Impact assessment

One of HOT’s unique strengths is its direct access to schools, and through them, to the wider communities that they serve.

We know anecdotally that there is a positive ripple effect from our work on student aspirations in each of our 5 Member schools. This is not simply an incidental added value but is an integral element in our model and philosophy. The HOT programme is about delivering change and opportunity for students, our partner schools and their communities.

It is therefore vital that we can capture data and map impacts within a clear research framework. However, we do not presently have the resources to adequately and systematically achieve this. Designing the framework, researching, and analysing data to present objective impact evidence is a task that will require a new dedicated resource.

Total one-off cost £25K

6 Student and alumni well-being coaching and support

The work of our programme managers and the impact of the student mentoring initiative has been vitally important in serving students adjusting to radically new and challenging life circumstances. Helping to develop personal resilience, countering imposter syndrome, and dispelling the understandable fear of the new, are all areas where mentoring has proven hugely beneficial and successful.

However, we have also learned that there is an unmet need for a more personalised and deeper form of confidential support for students dealing with specific problems and challenges. We have trialled this form of personalised support recently with a package of seven sessions which proved to be highly successful. To extend this pilot into a permanent support service, we will need a designated reserve that can offer support for up to eight students p.a. when required.

Total of £30K over 5 years

7 HOT consultancy services - developing our Intervention Offer

A natural extension of HOT’s programme to broaden opportunities for more young people could be achieved through the creation of a new consultancy arm to collaborate with schools on initiatives to reduce the attainment gap based on our proven record of success.

Narrowing the ever-increasing attainment gap between students from areas of social disadvantage and their peers is a national challenge. The impact of Covid has increased that gap. We have a proven model that is narrowing that gap and could therefore be a model for school improvement beyond our partner schools. We want to share this good practice with other schools and their communities but to do this we need your help. We have developed a wealth of positive experience and a suite of good practice tools that can help raise aspirations in schools, and the support processes to enable that. The consultancy would be a means of expanding opportunities for schools and students, but would equally be a source of revenue for HOT helping to sustain and underpin our core programmes.

Designing and marketing the consultancy model, including engagement with target schools, and scoping the expertise and resources required, will need a development budget and additional professional resources at the start-up stage.

Development cost of £40K over 2 years

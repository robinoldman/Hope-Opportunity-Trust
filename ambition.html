Ambitions

Our success has given us the momentum and motivation to do more. To change more lives and create more opportunities for our young people. To that end, we have identified seven key opportunities, but to make them happen, we need to raise new independent funding. This would enable us to extend and enrich our work, impacting even more young people and their communities. We are asking for your support to make this happen.

Please contact: Our CEO, John, to discuss any aspect of our operations and ambition at john.denny@gmail.com or our Operations Director, Sue at sue.yatesy@btinternet.com

Our Six opportunities for expanding our impact are:

1. Direct access for students from the Northwest.

There is interest from private schools that would offer direct access for students if HOT could support the process. We know from experience that supporting five students and their families from selection to placement will require an increase in our unrestricted income. Please contact us for costs.

2. Developing Apprenticeship Opportunities to expand the HOT offer to our Member schools and the community .

Collaborating with larger organisations, we have been offered access to fully funded apprenticeships and supported university places for students who wish to pursue specific careers. To take this opportunity forward, we must fully support the students throughout the apprenticeship programme. This will take an additional part-time role with associated costs.

3 Parent mentoring programme

We have recognised the need to support families and guardians from the beginning of this bursary process. It is an investment vital to the boarding programme's success, recognising that many parents have difficulty adjusting and giving their children the space and support to take advantage of this life-changing educational opportunity.

We know from experience that close collaboration with all parties helps minimise the risk of the “pull” from parents and the wider home community to “stick to what they are familiar with” Without this support, parents can struggle to overcome their concerns about losing their child to a world they are unfamiliar with and unsure how to support their child within it. For this to succeed, it needs resourcing beyond the volunteer support that is currently available.

We want to develop a parent mentoring programme that mirrors the successful student mentoring programme, thereby linking new parents with mentors whose children have been through the process and are now employed.

4 - Aspire and engage programme

Our programme managers' work and the impact of the student mentoring initiative have been vitally important in serving students adjusting to radically new and challenging life circumstances. Helping to develop personal resilience, countering imposter syndrome, and dispelling the understandable fear of the unknown are all areas where mentoring has proven hugely beneficial and successful.

However, we have also learned an unmet need for more personalised and profound confidential support for students with specific problems and challenges. We have trialled this form of personalised support recently with a package of seven sessions, which proved highly successful. When required, we will need a designated reserve to support up to eight students, a year, to extend this pilot into a permanent support service.

‘After spending most of my childhood in Hungary, I moved to Liverpool with my mum and brother due to challenging family circumstances. Settling in a new country has not always been easy – my mum struggled to find employment when we first came to England but today now works as a care home assistant, something that she loves. I have also, through perseverance, found a path that I love. Soon after I arrived, I sat an exam to apply to King’s Leadership Academy and I was lucky enough to get in and continue the next two years of my studies there. In my last year, I became a Prefect and made friends who I keep in contact with to this day. I loved being part of that community. It was this school’s partnership with the Royal National Children's SpringBoard Foundation that allowed me to take the opportunity of becoming a boarding school student at Marlborough College for my A-levels.​

One particularly memorable trip from my experience of joining the RNCSF programme was a visit to St. Peter’s School in York where I got a feeling of what boarding schools are like. At the end of this trip my teacher from King’s Leadership also showed us around parts of the University of York. It was my first time seeing and being shown around a university, which inspired me to seek higher education and aspire to become the first person in my family to go to university

The shift from studying at a state school to living in a boarding school four hours away from home has been a big change. However, it has allowed me to have opportunities - such as a trip to NASA - which I still think of as the best experience of my life. On top of that, I have made lifelong friends at Marlborough, not only in my boarding house with the girls I live with on a day-to-day basis, but also with my classmates and other pupils with whom I share numerous interests. I am the Head of Charities and Sustainability in my house and have organised fundraising events for a wide range of causes, such as Bobby’s Fund and Alzheimer’s Research UK. Since I am lucky to be supported by my school and the Royal National Children's SpringBoard Foundation, it is great to raise money for people who are disadvantaged because of different circumstances.

In an academic sense, Marlborough has provided a lot of inspiration which has helped me to determine what I want to do later in life. For example, a talk given by Dr. Chiara Marletto earlier this year about quantum gravity has sparked my interest in more complex branches of quantum and classical physics. It was a privilege to be taught by a professional like Dr. Marletto and it was also an inspiration to see a woman on the frontline of scientific discovery. Another talk, this one given by a pupil in my school who has taught himself relativity, was also a great joy to attend. It was uplifting to share his enthusiasm about the subject, and it sticks in my mind as a great example of the supportive and collaborative nature of this subject at its best, which I hope to experience more widely in the years to come when I study physics at university.
